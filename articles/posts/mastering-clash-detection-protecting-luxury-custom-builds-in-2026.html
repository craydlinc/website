Soft Clashes: The Invisible Conflicts of Clearance and Code

Soft clashes are more subtle but equally dangerous. They occur when an object violates a spatial requirement or safety buffer. High-end builds require dedicated clearance zones for mechanical servicing and electrical panel access. If a water heater is tucked so tightly into a closet that a technician cannot reach the pilot light, you’ve triggered a soft clash. According to 2024 field data, these violations are the #1 cause of failed building inspections in custom luxury homes. This BIM clash detection guide highlights how these buffers protect the long-term viability of the structure. By modeling these “invisible” zones, we ensure your project meets every code requirement the first time. You can optimize your design workflow to account for these buffers instantly, ensuring maintenance crews have the “elbow room” they need for years to come.

Workflow Clashes: Solving the Logistics Puzzle

The third pillar involves 4D conflicts, also known as workflow clashes. These aren’t about physical objects hitting each other, but about time and sequence. If the drywall crew is scheduled to close a ceiling before the electrical inspector has signed off on the wiring, you have a workflow clash. Virtual Design and Construction (VDC) prevents “trade stacking,” a chaotic scenario where too many subcontractors occupy the same room simultaneously. This congestion drops productivity by as much as 30% and increases safety risks. We integrate delivery schedules directly into the digital coordination model.
